## Key Site Facts (Must Be Accurately Represented)

### SSSI Ecological Significance
- **Status**: Site of Special Scientific Interest
- **Habitat**: Most extensive semi-natural downland in Greater London
- **Soils**: Thin rendzina soils on chalk slopes

### Key Species
| Category | Species | Significance |
|----------|---------|--------------|
| Orchids | 8 species including **man orchid** | Nationally scarce |
| Plants | **Greater yellow-rattle** | Largest British colony |
| Plants | **Round-headed rampion** | Nationally scarce |
| Plants | Horseshoe vetch, sainfoin, hairy violet | Restricted London distribution |
| Birds | Skylark, yellowhammer, nightingale | Indicator species |
| Mammals | Roe deer, dormouse | Notable populations |
| Butterflies | 30+ species | Chalk grassland specialists |

### Heritage Features
- Ancient yew trees (parish/property boundary markers)
- Saxon burial mounds (tumuli)
- Celtic field boundaries
- Neolithic trackways
- Devilsden Wood (ancient woodland)

### Nature Trail
- 29 stops (some sources say 116 individual points)
- Audio narration by **Roger McGough CBE**
- Available in **4 languages**
- QR code accessible
