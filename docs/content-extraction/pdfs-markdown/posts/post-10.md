# Post 10: Eastern Hedge

## Tree and Shrub Spotting

Follow this path through the trees for approximately 450 metres. See how many different types of tree and shrub you can spot between this post and the next one. Are there more species in this hedge than at post 4?

**How many points did you score?**

## Trees and Shrubs to Spot

### Ash (5 points)
Light green leaflets in pairs, each up to 10cm, bark smooth & silvery, buds black.

### Hazel (5 points)
Round, pointed-tipped leaves with softly prickly hairs, clusters of green nuts in autumn.

### Yew (10 points) - POISONOUS
Dark green, flattened needles, bark red & flaking, pinkish-red fruit on some trees in late summer.

### Oak (10 points)
Short-stalked, deeply lobed leaves, with acorns on long stalks in the autumn.

### Field Maple (15 points)
Leaves (4-8cm) deep green, bark corky, two-winged seeds like 'helicopter blades'.

### Beech (15 points)
Leaves thin & wavy-edged, yellow-green when young, turning dark green and then brown in autumn. Thin twigs and pointed, cigar-shaped buds.

### Holly (15 points)
Tough, shiny, dark green evergreen leaves with sharp spines. Red berries in autumn.

### Elder (10 points)
Bark deeply furrowed on older trees, large pores on younger branches. Clusters of pale yellow flowers in June, black berries in late summer.

### Silver Birch (15 points)
White, scaly bark with black patches. Leaves almost triangular, toothed with smaller teeth between those formed by the main veins.

### Sycamore (10 points)
Large dark green leaves (up to 15cm) with 5 pointed, lobed leaves, two-winged 'helicopter' seeds at right angles.
