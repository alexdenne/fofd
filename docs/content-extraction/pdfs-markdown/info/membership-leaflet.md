# Friends of Farthing Downs & Happy Valley

## About Us

The Friends was formed in **2005** with the full support of the City of London who have owned and managed Farthing Downs for the benefit of the public since **1883**. It is a fully constituted body led by a committee of volunteers who meet regularly with representatives from the City.

---

## About Farthing Downs

### Protected Status
- **Green Belt**
- **Site of Special Scientific Interest (SSSI)**
- **Scheduled Ancient Monument**

### Natural Features
- Unique chalk grassland
- Home to wild flowers including the **rare Greater Yellow Rattle** and **rare orchids**
- Increasingly threatened species of birds and insects

### Historic Features
- Distinctive Celtic field systems
- Saxon burial tumuli
- Neolithic trackways
- WWII anti-aircraft trenches

### Access
- Open space with free access
- Woodland, open downland, and views of local and distant landmarks
- Many footpaths and bridleways

### Signposted Walks
- London Loop
- Downland Circular Walk
- Farthing Downs and Happy Valley Nature Trail

## The Role of the Friends

The Friends work with the City of London to:
- Protect the heritage of Farthing Downs
- Promote the value of the Downs
- Initiate and support community projects

Funds raised are used towards:
- Leaflets and Information boards
- Organising walks and talks
- Local environmental projects

---

## Aims and Objectives

### Aims
- Enhance the heritage and leisure value of Farthing Downs & New Hill
- Promote awareness and interest through events and activities for all ages and communities
- Protect the integrity of the Downs by working with the City of London

### Objectives
- Organise local events
- Keep members appraised through Newsletters, Leaflets, and Website
- Organise walks and talks on Heritage and Natural History
- Encourage community/visitor involvement through exhibitions
- Fundraise to support projects
