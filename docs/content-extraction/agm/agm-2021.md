# AGM 2021 - Tree Symposium

## Shaping Up To Climate Change

**"Who's for More Trees Symposium"**

Speakers:
- **Richard Edwards** - LB Croydon
- **Tom Oliver** - City of London
- **David Hatcher** - Woodland Trust

### Symposium Report

The full symposium report covers the three speeches and expands to include:
- Climate Change issues
- Current Planning Rules
- Open Spaces management

A comprehensive "Symposium Report" document was produced by the Friends, available as a PDF.

### Key Themes

**Climate Action:**
- Role of trees in carbon capture
- Urban forestry strategies
- Woodland creation and management
- Biodiversity protection

**Planning and Policy:**
- Integration of climate considerations in planning
- Protection of existing woodland
- Tree planting targets
- Long-term management strategies

**Local Implementation:**
- Croydon's tree strategy
- City of London's woodland management
- Woodland Trust's national perspective
- Community engagement in tree planting

The symposium represented a significant focus on environmental action and the role of trees in addressing climate change, building on the challenges identified during the COVID-19 pandemic.
