Guest Speaker

Councillor Scott Roche

Cabinet Member for Streets and Environment

A Strategic Approach to Biodiversity and Open Spaces in Croydon

Chairman's Report - Graham Lomas

Nature Trail Completion

Through 2020-2021, refurbishment of Happy Valley and Farthing Downs Nature Trail completed:

  • New posts and QR codes
  • Audio commentary by 15 celebrities
  • Booklet rewritten and updated in full color
  • Translations into French, Hindi and Polish (completed 2021)

2021 Tree Symposium

  • Presentations by Richard Edwards (Croydon), Thomas Oliver (City of London), David Hatcher (Woodland Trust)
  • Booklet produced: 'Shaping up to Climate Change' (available June 2022)
  • Available online at www.friendsoffarthingdowns.co.uk/AGM2021.html

AONB Extension Proposal

Natural England proposals for South London Downs NNR to be included in extended Area of Outstanding Natural Beauty covering Surrey Hills. Chairman made submission supporting SLDNNR evaluation for AONB status.

Councillor Scott Roche: Croydon's Open Spaces

Cllr Roche is councillor for Shirley South, living close to important open spaces.

Cabinet Member Responsibilities

Streets and Environment Portfolio:

  • Road Safety
  • Air Quality
  • Transport
  • Open Spaces
  • Waste Management

Email Volume: Increased from 20/day to 200/day - aim to follow up on all queries.

Friends of Groups Engagement

Site Visits:

  • Aim to visit all 47 'Friends of' Groups (FoGs) in Borough
  • Visited 35 groups since May
  • Exploring issues and problems unique to each site
  • Interest in Friends groups growing in North Borough creating own voice

Practical Issues

Grass Cutting
  • Major problem identified
  • Proposals to re-establish service
  • Issues: larger mowers required, two broken, shortage of drivers
  • Need to inform residents why Nature Reserve grass not cut
Re-wilding and Wildflower Meadows
  • More expertise needed
  • Wildflowers grow in nutrient-poor soil
  • Some projects not successful

Insurance and Funding

Insurance
  • Major problem for FoGs (¬£150/annum cost)
  • Borough-wide liability insurance mooted
  • Investigation requested: Can Community Infrastructure Levy (CIL) be used for insurance?
Section 106 Money Allocated
  • ¬£200,000 for green spaces
  • ¬£100,000 for trees
  • FoGs can be involved in decision making
  • Need to identify what and where
  • Limit on notification time (developers can reclaim funds)

Key Discussion Points

Q: Biodiversity

Ecologist needed in Planning Department. Developers destroying badger setts. 'Brick wall' between ecologists and planners who need to be 'more at one'.

A: Agreed - interrelationship needed between planning procedures and wildlife protection. Mayor currently looking at intensification strategy and how it relates to wildlife at local level.

Q: Biodiversity Strategy

Collapse of biodiversity is real. Strategy needed to know what we've got.

A: Lot of reports on biodiversity in Borough need looking at. Data and information needed. Strategy will be developed by officers.

Cllr Roche Summary

Staffing Crisis
  • Severe reduction in staff
  • Number of vacancies waiting to be filled
  • Resulting reduction in services
  • Required service levels difficult to meet
Forum Needed
  • For FoGs to share experiences, information and knowledge
  • Current feedback: frustration, distance from Council, lack of action and communication
  • Sympathy for current situation needing understanding and action

The Chairman thanked Cllr Roche for his informative and frank presentation and for answering questions.
