Resources & Documents

Strategy papers, wildlife records, and conservation research

Reference Library

Over nearly 20 years, Friends of Farthing Downs & Happy Valley has produced strategy papers, consultation responses, and wildlife surveys. These documents form a valuable resource for anyone interested in conservation policy, local planning issues, and the natural history of the South London Downs.

Conservation & Biodiversity

Discussion papers and strategy documents addressing biodiversity net gain, climate change, and the conservation challenges facing local authorities in managing green spaces.

Where Net Zero Morphs Into Net Gain

Author: Graham Lomas, Chair
Date: November 2023

Background briefing on the two major challenges facing local authorities: achieving net zero carbon emissions by 2050, and implementing Biodiversity Net Gain under the Environment Act 2021. Examines Surrey's 63 Biodiversity Opportunity Areas and Croydon's 127 open spaces.

Key topics: BNG legislation, site substitution, Surrey Nature Partnership, habitat compensation, local authority capacity

Request this document

Strategic Approach to Biodiversity and Open Spaces

Author: Graham Lomas, Chairman
Date: October 2022

Comprehensive discussion paper on managing Croydon's 127 open spaces covering 1,550 hectares. Proposes seven strategic approaches including community forums, ecological focus, smarter departmental working, and new management models like the Streatham Common Co-operative.

Key topics: Community Infrastructure Levy, volunteer coordination, GreenSpace Information database, South London NNR, financial sustainability

Request this document

Who's For More Trees?

Author: Graham M Lomas
Date: November 2021

Symposium background paper examining tree disease impacts (ash dieback, Dutch elm disease), carbon capture targets requiring 30 million new trees annually, and balancing woodland management with biodiversity on the City Commons. Questions whether suburban intensification conflicts with greening ambitions.

Key topics: Tree diseases, carbon neutral Britain by 2050, Woodland Trust strategy, suburban development pressures, City Commons management

Request this document

Policy & Planning

Documents addressing Green Belt protection, AONB boundary extensions, local plan consultations, and the ongoing challenge of protecting green spaces from development pressure.

Extending Protection for Major Open Spaces

Author: Graham Lomas, Chair
Date: March 2023

Analysis of Natural England's consultation to extend the Surrey Hills Area of Outstanding Natural Beauty to include Happy Valley, Chipstead, and Caterham Woods. Discusses the 16-month boundary variation study proposing 100 sq km expansion and the challenge of balancing landscape quality against urban development proximity.

Key topics: AONB designation, Surrey Hills expansion, landscape protection criteria, Natural England consultation, North Downs conservation

Request this document

Justifying Local Green Spaces in Coulsdon

Organization: Friends of Farthing Downs & Happy Valley
Type: Community consultation campaign

Resident advocacy campaign responding to the Croydon Local Plan inspection demanding stronger justification for retaining local green spaces. Identifies 14 sites at risk including Coulsdon Memorial Ground, Chaldon Way Gardens, Coulsdon Coppice, and Rickman Hill Rec, with response forms for community input.

Key topics: Local plan consultation, green space protection, development pressure, resident advocacy, community character

Request this document

Valuing Your Local Environment

Type: Photo display and educational leaflet
Venue: Coulsdon South Station

Public engagement material explaining Farthing Downs' status as the highest point in Greater London, its designation as Green Belt, SSSI, and Scheduled Ancient Monument. Discusses historical context of the City of London's 1883 purchase and modern development pressures from London's planned growth of 3 million more people by 2036.

Key topics: Heritage conservation, North Downs geology, ancient monuments, urban growth pressures, landscape protection status

Request this document

Wildlife Records

Species surveys and wildlife observations from Farthing Downs & Happy Valley, contributing to our understanding of the site's biodiversity and helping monitor ecological health over time.

Moth Night Species List 2015

Date: 25th July 2015
Event: Friends of Farthing Downs Moth Night

Complete species list from the 2015 Moth Night survey recording 22 macro moth species and 5 micro moth species, plus observations of caddis flies, ichneumon wasps, glow-worms, and pipistrelle bats. Notable species include Pretty Chalk Carpet, Beautiful Hook-tip, and Chocolate Tip.

Community & Membership

Information about joining the Friends, getting involved in conservation work, and celebrating the community members who have made lasting contributions to protecting the Downs.

Membership Information

Type: Membership leaflet
Updated: Regularly revised

Complete guide to joining the Friends of Farthing Downs & Happy Valley, including information about the organization's history since 2005, achievements timeline, aims and objectives, ways to get involved in walks, talks, exhibitions and conservation work, and membership fees (Individual ¬£5, Family ¬£8 per year).

Elizabeth Brooks Memorial

1953-2022
Type: Memorial fundraising appeal

Tribute to Lizzie Brooks, who founded the Friends Facebook group and served on the committee from 2018. An active campaigner for the Tollers Estate and local environment, described as "a breath of fresh air." Memorial donations funded the replacement of the Nature Trail Information Board at the Interpretation Mound near the car park on Farthing Downs.

Legacy: Facebook group founder, committee member, Tollers Estate bus advocate, environmental activist, Old Coulsdon Camera Club member
