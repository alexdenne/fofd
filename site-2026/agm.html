A decade of Annual General Meetings featuring expert speakers on conservation, biodiversity, and protecting our precious green spaces.

Our Annual Meetings

Each November, Friends of Farthing Downs & Happy Valley gather to review the year's work and hear from guest speakers on topics vital to our mission: protecting and promoting these ancient landscapes for future generations.

AGM 2024

6th November 2024

Guest Speakers: Richard Biggs (Leader, Reigate and Banstead Council), Richard Knox-Johnson (Chair, London Green Belt Council), Tom Oliver (Conservation Officer, City of London)

Topic: Housing, Green Belt, Brownfield, Grey Field - A Discussion on Government Policy

Panel discussion examining the complex challenges of government housing targets, Green Belt protection, Grey Belt designation, and the case for prioritizing brownfield development. Speakers explored the pressures facing local planning authorities, the vulnerabilities of London's Green Belt, and the economic and environmental benefits of preserving green spaces.

Green Belt protection Grey Belt designation Brownfield development Housing targets

AGM 2023

8th November 2023

Guest Speaker: Mike Waite MCIEEM (Director of Research and Planning, Surrey Wildlife Trust; Chair of Surrey Nature Partnership Biodiversity Working Group)

Topic: Biodiversity Net Gain

Mike explained the new 2024 Biodiversity Net Gain legislation requiring all planning applications to deliver measurable improvements for biodiversity. He covered habitat metrics, the mitigation hierarchy, local nature recovery strategies, and the challenge of implementing BNG with limited skilled staff in local authorities.

Biodiversity Net Gain Planning legislation Habitat metrics Nature recovery strategies

AGM 2022

9th November 2022

Guest Speaker: Councillor Scott Roche (Cabinet Member for Streets and Environment)

Topic: A Strategic Approach to Biodiversity and Open Spaces in Croydon

Cllr Roche discussed his vision for engaging with Croydon's 47 'Friends of' groups, practical issues like grass cutting and wildflower meadows, Section 106 funding opportunities, and the need for better coordination between planning and biodiversity protection. He acknowledged severe staffing challenges while emphasizing the vital role of volunteer groups.

Biodiversity strategy Council services Friends groups support Section 106 funding

AGM 2021

10th November 2021

Guest Speakers: Richard Edwards (LB Croydon), Tom Oliver (City of London), David Hatcher (Woodland Trust)

Topic: Tree Symposium - "Who's for More Trees" - Shaping Up To Climate Change

A special symposium exploring the role of trees in carbon capture, urban forestry strategies, woodland creation and management, and biodiversity protection. The comprehensive report examined climate change issues, current planning rules, and open spaces management from local and national perspectives.

Climate change Tree planting Woodland management Carbon sequestration

AGM 2020

15th-31st December 2020

Format: Online AGM (COVID-19 pandemic) - Email and postal voting

Topic: Reports distributed online - Nature Trail completion, Croydon Council insolvency, South London Downs NNR

A year like no other. With in-person meetings impossible, the Friends distributed comprehensive reports online covering the completion of the acclaimed Nature Trail project with celebrity narrators, the shock of Croydon Council's insolvency, designation of the South London Downs National Nature Reserve, and reflections on how COVID-19 changed our relationship with green spaces.

COVID-19 pandemic response Nature Trail completion Croydon Council insolvency South London Downs NNR

AGM 2019

6th November 2019

Guest Speaker: Jonathan Halls (Artist, Archivist and Illustrator)

Topic: Wilderness in the City

Jonathan presented his creative work exploring urban wilderness, particularly railway nature corridors. Through art, installations and his 'Croydon Railway Nature Trail' project, he challenged assumptions about green space definitions and demonstrated how railway lands could link nature reserves. He highlighted concerns about Network Rail's vegetation management and the need for environmental expertise.

Urban wilderness Railway nature corridors Green space definitions Network Rail vegetation management

AGM 2018

7th November 2018

Guest Speakers: Councillor Oliver Lewis (Cabinet Member) and Kelvin Shewry (Live Well Programme Manager)

Topic: Moving Forward from Masterplanning - Destination Parks

Cllr Lewis announced Natural England's proposal to designate the South London Downs National Nature Reserve, one of the highest protections for open spaces. He outlined implementation of the Destination Parks Masterplan for Happy Valley, including accessibility improvements and natural play areas, while emphasizing the vital partnership role of Friends groups as local authority resources dwindle.

Destination Parks Masterplan National Nature Reserve Natural play areas Partnership working

AGM 2017

8th November 2017

Guest Speakers: Councillor Timothy Godfrey and Juliet Stevenson (Live Well Co-ordinator)

Topic: Croydon's 'Ambitious for Parks Project'

Against a backdrop of severe Council funding cuts (80-90% reduction in government grants), Cllr Godfrey presented the Ambitious for Parks programme and Tyrens survey of Happy Valley. He emphasized that parks have no statutory protection and announced the National Nature Reserve designation. Juliet presented Croydon's Live Well health programme linking green spaces to community wellbeing.

Ambitious for Parks Happy Valley future Public health Council budget cuts

AGM 2016

9th November 2016

Guest Speaker: Dominic North (Ranger, Happy Valley)

Topic: A Guided Tour of Happy Valley

Dominic provided an in-depth look at Happy Valley's ecology, from its Ice Age formation to modern conservation challenges. He described rare orchids (including endangered Fly and Man orchids), the success of sheep grazing schemes, dormouse monitoring, ancient woodland management through coppicing, and the vital work of volunteers. A celebration of why Happy Valley deserves its special protection.

Happy Valley ecology Chalk grassland management Woodland conservation Rare species protection

AGM 2015

11th November 2015

Guest Speaker: Christine Howard (Chairman, Surrey Hills Society)

Topic: Protecting Our Open Spaces: The Next 20 Years

Christine explored the challenges facing the Surrey Hills AONB - England's second designated Area of Outstanding Natural Beauty. She discussed population pressure from London's growth, development threats from airport expansion and fracking, the funding gap between National Parks and AONBs (receiving just ¬£6M versus ¬£53M annually), and creative solutions through farm diversification and community engagement.

Surrey Hills AONB Green Belt protection Rural landscape management Future threats

AGM 2014

November 2014

Guest Speaker: Sue Ireland (Director Open Spaces, City of London)

Topic: Maintaining City Open Spaces Round London - the challenges ahead

Sue outlined the City of London's management of 11,000 acres of open spaces around London, including the Coulsdon Commons. She discussed conservation challenges including tree diseases (Massaria fungus, Chalara ash dieback), visitor management conflicts, and significant financial pressures with a ¬£17M deficit expected. She emphasized the essential role of volunteers and partnership working to maintain these precious spaces.

City open spaces funding Conservation challenges Tree diseases Climate change adaptation
