Walks & Trails

From gentle strolls to longer rambles, discover ancient trackways and breathtaking views across the North Downs.

The Circular Walk

Distance: 2 miles | Time: 1 hour | Difficulty: Easy

Perfect for families. Loop around the main downs taking in the viewpoint, Saxon burial mounds, and wildflower meadows. Mostly flat with gentle slopes.

Safety Information

Farthing Downs is open access land managed for wildlife. Please follow these guidelines to protect yourself and the environment.

üêï

Dogs on Leads

During bird nesting season (March-July), dogs must be kept on leads to protect ground-nesting birds. We recommend leads year-round near grazing cattle.

ü¶ü

Tick & Lyme Disease Awareness

Ticks live in long grass and can carry Lyme disease. Protect yourself: wear long trousers tucked into socks, long sleeves, and closed shoes when walking through meadows. Check yourself thoroughly for tick bites after your visit and remove any ticks promptly with fine-tipped tweezers. If you develop a circular rash or flu-like symptoms after a tick bite, see NHS guidance on Lyme disease.

üêÑ

Grazing Cattle

Cattle graze the Downs to maintain the chalk grassland. Give them space, don't approach calves, and keep dogs under close control near livestock.

Weather & Terrain

Parts of the Downs are exposed with little shade. Bring sun protection in summer and warm layers in winter. Paths can be muddy - wear appropriate footwear.

Emergency Contact

In an emergency, call 999. For non-urgent issues, contact the City of London Rangers on 020 8668 1286
